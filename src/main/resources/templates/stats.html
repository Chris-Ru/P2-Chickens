The Outbreak of Senioritis: An Overview

Our Mission:

A common problem that is negatively affecting the grades of Del Norte High School students is claimed to have
been infecting this year's graduating senior class. What is the name of this mystery disease?
Senioritis - an ailment that seems to negatively affect the attitude and grades of DNHS Seniors.
Seniors, who have already finished their college applications, feel like they have accomplished everything they need to
for the year, and don't feel as much pressure to continue striving for academic excellence. We wanted to prove whether or not seniors
experience a decrease in motivation for obtaining and keeping good grades, in contrast to their fellow underclassmen.
In our study, we asked 48 seniors and 42 non-seniors to rate their motivation level on a scale of 1-10.

What is this? and Why do we need this?

Because of the negative consequences that a lower motivation has on a student, proving that
Senioritis is a legitimate disease can help in preventing its harmful effects. Falling into the belief that
seniors don't need to try for grades can cause a lack of motivation and careless behavior for seniors.
This may affect their mental health and physical health, harming the student's attitude towards school.
We want to raise awareness for future classes of Del Norte seniors, so they may be able to immunize themselves against Senioritis.
